## Design Context

### Users
PixivDL is used by a local desktop user who is already logged into Pixiv in Chrome and wants to quickly fetch one artwork by PID, inspect all pages, select images, download files or ZIPs, and maintain a small local favorites gallery.

### Brand Personality
Clear, quiet, precise. The interface should feel like a focused browser tool rather than a marketing page or a heavy desktop app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined minimal utility in a blue-white palette. White and cool blue surfaces should create trust and clarity; Pixiv artwork thumbnails provide the visual richness. Avoid dark shells, red-heavy accents, decorative gradients, card clutter, and generic dashboard styling.

### Design Principles
- Put the current task first: PID search, image selection, and download controls should be visually separate.
- Let images carry the interface; controls should stay calm and compact.
- Use blue as the action/state color and reserve it for important affordances.
- Favorites are a gallery, not a settings panel; the new-tab view should feel open and browsable.
- Avoid storing or exposing Pixiv cookies; communicate status without adding noise.
